What this dashboard is Start here

A shared sales intelligence tool for the brightwheel Gov/ECE team — built to track outreach to early childhood directors across FL, AL, GA, MI, ID, NV, NM, AZ, UT, CO, CA, OR, and WA.

The dashboard gives each rep a live view of 1,371 districts, ranked by buying signals, enrollment size, and recency of contact. Every tab is filtered to your rep's states by default, but you can switch the filter to see all reps.

What it tracks

Emails sent, replies received, Granola call notes, physical mailers, calls, and manually logged activities — all shared in real time across the team.

Connecting Gmail

Gmail is needed to send emails, create drafts, sync your sent activity, and load the shared team activity log.

  1. Click Sign in with Gmail in the top-right corner of the dashboard.
  2. Choose your @mybrightwheel.com Google account and grant the requested permissions.
  3. Once connected, your name and rep icon will appear in the header. The dashboard will automatically sync your sent history and load the team activity log.
You'll need to reconnect Gmail each session — but your activity data loads from your browser instantly on every visit, so you won't lose any history between sessions.

Connecting Granola

Granola is a meeting notes app. Connecting it lets the dashboard pull your call notes and match them to districts automatically.

  1. Click Connect Granola in the top-right corner.
  2. In the modal that appears, paste your Granola API key. You can find this in the Granola desktop app under Settings → API.
  3. Click Connect. The dashboard will immediately fetch your recent meeting notes and match them to districts.
  4. Matched meetings appear in the relevant district's activity timeline in the District Info tab.

How matching works

The dashboard tries to match each Granola note to a district using three approaches, in order:

  • District name — the meeting title or notes mention a district name
  • Email domain — an email address in the notes belongs to a known district contact
  • Director last name — the meeting title contains a director's last name alongside a call keyword (call, meeting, demo, sync, etc.)
Once connected, click Sync Granola in the header at any time to pull new notes. The dashboard won't re-log meetings it has already matched.

Overview tab Home

A high-level summary of pipeline health and recent activity across your districts.

Stats bar

The header shows total districts, hot leads, warm leads, number contacted, and items in the Send Queue.

Rep filter

The All Reps dropdown in the top bar filters the entire dashboard — Overview, Prospects, and Contact Tracking — to a single rep's states. Switching reps updates all three tabs at once.

Priority tiers

🔥 Hot

Strong buying signals, large enrollment, recent board activity.

🌡️ Warm

Some signals or medium enrollment. Worth reaching out soon.

Cool

Smaller districts or fewer signals. Lower immediate priority.

Cold

Minimal signals and small enrollment.

Prospects tab

A sortable, filterable table of all districts. The main place to find, research, and queue outreach.

Filters

  • Search — searches district name, director name, and county.
  • Priority — filter to Hot / Warm / Cool / Cold tiers.
  • State — filter to a single state.
  • Curriculum — filter by current curriculum vendor.
  • Status — filter by outreach stage (Not Started, Email Sent, VM Left, Responded, etc.).
  • Rep filter (top bar) — shows only states owned by the selected rep.

Queueing emails

Each district row has an Add to Queue button. Click it to choose an email template and add the district to the Send Queue. From there you can review, edit, and send.

Selecting multiple districts

Check the box on any district row to select it. Once you have multiple selected, a bulk action bar appears at the bottom — letting you add all selected districts to the queue with one template in one click.

Districts with a bounced or unsubscribed email address are automatically skipped during bulk sends. You'll see a warning if you try to manually queue them individually.

Contact Tracking tab

A per-director view of every touchpoint — emails, calls, replies, mailers, and notes — across the team.

Each district shows a timeline of all logged activity: Gmail sends, Gmail replies, Granola call notes, and manually logged activities. The contact count is the total number of distinct touchpoints.

Outreach stage badges

Each district displays its current stage in the outreach sequence. Stages update automatically when Gmail sync detects a send or reply, or you can set them manually from the district row.

Logging activity manually

Expand any district and click Log Activity to record a call, LinkedIn message, meeting, or note. Choose the type, date, and add notes. This saves to your browser and syncs to the shared team log so teammates see it when they connect.

Syncing Gmail

Click Sync Gmail at the top of the Contact Tracking tab to pull new sent emails and replies from the last 90 days. Any email sent to a known district contact will appear as an activity. Gmail sync also detects bounces automatically.

Call window indicator

Once 5 days have passed since the initial email was sent and the district hasn't moved past the Email Sent stage, the dashboard flags it as ready for a call. This is part of the Summer Outreach sequence cadence.

District Info tab

A detailed profile for a single district — director info, buying signals, board notes, contact history, and email composer.

Click any district name in Prospects or Contact Tracking to open its full profile here.

What's shown

  • Director info — name, title, email, phone, LinkedIn
  • Buying signals — reasons this district is a strong prospect
  • Recent intel — board meeting notes and strategic context
  • Granola notes — matched call notes from your Granola account
  • Activity history — all logged contacts in chronological order
  • Email composer — preview and send any template directly from this view

Summer Bridge contact

Some FL districts have a separate Summer Bridge contact. Summer Bridge email templates will be addressed to that contact instead of the main director. All other templates still go to the director.

Send Queue tab

A staging area where queued emails wait for review before sending.

  1. Add districts to the queue from the Prospects tab using Add to Queue, or bulk-add multiple at once.
  2. Switch to the Send Queue tab to review each email — subject, preview text, and recipient are shown for each item.
  3. Click Send to send via Gmail, or Draft to save it as a Gmail draft you can review in your inbox first.
  4. Sent emails are automatically logged as activities in Contact Tracking and synced to the team log.
Use Draft instead of Send if you want to add a personal touch before it goes out. The draft will appear in your Gmail Drafts folder.

Sending all drafts at once

The All to Drafts button at the top of the Send Queue pushes every queued email to Gmail Drafts in one click. Useful when you want to personalize a batch before sending.

Email Copy tab Editors only

A central place to view and edit all outreach templates. Changes apply immediately to every email sent from the Send Queue.

All five reps can view templates here. Christie, Eric Truog, Sudeepta, Kevin, and Eric B can also edit them — a green ✓ You can edit badge appears in the header when you're signed in with editing rights.

Editing a built-in template

  1. Find the template you want to update and click Edit.
  2. Update the subject line and body. Use the token buttons ([First Name], [State Name], etc.) to insert dynamic placeholders at your cursor position.
  3. Click Save. The change is shared with the team immediately via the shared activity log.

Edited templates show an Edited by [name] · [date] tag so the team knows when copy was last changed and by whom. You can always click Reset to revert a template to its default copy.

Creating a custom template

Click the ✨ New Template button (visible to editors) to create a brand-new template from scratch. Give it a name, specify the audience or states it's intended for, write the subject and body, then click Save Template. Custom templates appear below the built-in ones and can be edited or deleted at any time.

Available tokens

  • [First Name] — director's first name
  • [State Name] — full state name (e.g. "Florida")
  • [District Name] — district name
  • [Calendly Link] — the sending rep's scheduling link
  • [Learn More Link] — the brightwheel learn-more page link
Template edits are synced to the shared team log, so everyone picks up the latest copy the next time they connect Gmail.

Outreach Sequence

The dashboard tracks each district through a structured 14-day outreach sequence — the Summer Outreach campaign.

Sequence stages

Each district moves through these stages as outreach progresses:

  • Not Started — no outreach yet
  • Email Sent — initial email delivered (Day 0)
  • Mailer Queued — physical mailer sent (Day 3)
  • VM Left — call placed, voicemail left (Day 5)
  • Follow-up Sent — post-call email sent (Day 7)
  • Closing Sent — final closing email sent (Day 12)
  • Responded ✓ — district replied or scheduled a call
  • Nurture — not ready now, follow up later

How stages update

Stages update automatically when Gmail sync detects a sent email or reply. You can also set a stage manually from the Prospects or Contact Tracking tabs by clicking the stage badge on a district row.

Call window

When 5 or more days have passed since the first outbound email and the district is still at Email Sent or Mailer Queued, the dashboard flags that district as ready for a call. Look for the call indicator in the Contact Tracking tab.

Personalized Emails

For districts with enough research on file, the dashboard can generate a tailored email that references specific buying signals and recent intel.

When adding a district to the Send Queue, you'll see a Personalized option if the district has sufficient intel (buying signals, board notes, or recent news). This generates a one-off draft that's unique to that district rather than using a shared template.

Personalized emails are built from the district's buying signals and recent intel, and are automatically signed with your rep profile. They go through the same Send Queue review flow — you can edit before sending or save as a Gmail draft.

If a district doesn't have enough intel on file for a personalized email, the option won't appear. Use one of the standard templates instead.

Bounces & Unsubscribes

The dashboard automatically tracks delivery failures and opt-outs so you don't accidentally send to bad addresses.

Bounce detection

During Gmail sync, the dashboard scans your inbox for mailer-daemon and postmaster messages. If a bounce is detected for a known district contact, that email address is flagged. You'll see a warning if you try to send to a bounced address, and they are silently skipped during bulk sends.

Unsubscribes

All emails sent from the dashboard include an unsubscribe link in the footer. When a contact clicks it, their email is recorded in the shared activity log and flagged in the dashboard. Like bounces, unsubscribed contacts are silently skipped in bulk sends. If you try to queue one individually, you'll see a confirmation prompt before it goes through.

Bounces and unsubscribes are shared across the team — if Christie's sync detects a bounce, Kevin and Eric B will see it flagged too the next time they load the shared log.

Gmail Sync

Automatically detects emails you've sent to district contacts and replies you've received — even emails sent outside the dashboard.

What gets synced

  • Sent emails — any email sent to a known district director email address in the last 90 days
  • Replies — any email received from a known district contact in the last 90 days
  • Bounces — delivery failure notifications from mailer-daemon or postmaster in the last 90 days

What doesn't get synced

  • Emails to contacts not in the district database
  • Emails older than 90 days
  • Emails already synced in a previous session (so nothing gets double-counted)
Sync runs automatically every time you connect Gmail. You can also trigger it manually from the Contact Tracking tab.

Granola Sync

Pulls meeting notes from Granola and matches them to districts in the dashboard.

After connecting Granola, click Sync Granola in the header at any time to fetch new notes. The dashboard uses a fuzzy match on district name, email domain, and director last name to find the right district for each note.

Matched notes appear in the District Info tab under their own Granola section, separate from email activity. They also count toward the total contact count in the Contact Tracking tab.

Shared Activity Log

A shared log that keeps all reps' contact history in sync — so when Eric logs a call in Boise, Sudeepta sees it when she next connects Gmail.

Every time an activity is logged — via Gmail sync, Granola sync, manual entry, or a template edit — it's written to the shared log in the background. When you connect Gmail, the shared log loads first, populating the full team history before your own Gmail sync runs.

Bounces, unsubscribes, and template overrides are also stored in the shared log so they propagate across the whole team.

The shared log is append-only, so nothing ever gets overwritten. The same activity will never be counted twice, even if multiple reps sync the same email thread.

Data persistence

Your activity data is saved in two places: your browser (instant, always available) and the shared team log (syncs across reps when you connect Gmail).

Your browser

All activities, contact statuses, template edits, bounces, and unsubscribes are saved locally. Data survives page refreshes without needing to reconnect Gmail.

Shared team log

When you connect Gmail, the shared log merges everyone's activity into your view. Anything you log gets written back so teammates see it too.

If you switch to a new browser or clear your browser data, reconnecting Gmail and re-syncing will restore your full history from the shared team log.
